About Me

Hi! I'm a senior at MIT studying Computer Science and Mathematics. I am broadly interested in building computer vision systems that perceive the world as humans do, and learn general-purpose representations of the 3D world. Some topics of interest include representation learning, learning from synthetic data, scene understanding, and 3D perception. I have also worked in using insights from neuroscience and cognitive science to build better artificial intelligence systems.

I work at the MIT Center for Brains, Minds, and Machines with Pawan Sinha and Xavier Boix. In the past I've been fortunate to work at D. E. Shaw (reinforcement learning research), Apple (machine learning), and Two Sigma (software engineering). In my free time I enjoy hiking, writing, and running.
